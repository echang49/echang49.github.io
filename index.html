Hackathon Projects

Hack Western 2020 (London, Ontario)

Website that used the city of London's open data portal to predict future demographics of the city by neighbourhood through linear regression. Data points for the city map was seperated through a python script, the linear regression was completed through TensorFlow, a python library. The database for demographics were in the form of csv files. The project used a javascript library named leaflet.js to map the neighbourhoods on Google Maps.

Utilized: HTML5, CSS3, Javascript, Python, Leaflet.js, and Tensorflow

Citrus Hacks V (Online)

Website that lets either the farmer or customer log in through a secure and authenticated server in which the farmer is able to add products, and the customer is able to view all the products from all the farmers in their local area. The customer can then purchase this product and meet in a common point such as parking spot where the delivery will take place. The project was the winner in one of three main tracks of the hackathon.

Utilized: HTML5, Bootstrap, CSS3, JavaScript, Node.js, React, Express.js, Passport.js, MongoDB, and Mongoose.
